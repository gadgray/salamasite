Church Pastor

Rev. Dr. John Oliver Yakubu hails from Borno state Nigeria. He was called into the gospel ministry about 27 years ago. He had his Theological trainings from Baptist Theological Seminary, Kaduna (B.TH) in 2006; Master of Psychology and Biblical Counseling from ECWA Theological Seminary, Jos in 2013 and PhD in Pastoral Care and Counseling /World Religions in The Nigerian Baptist Theological Seminary, Ogbomoso in 2020. He is a gifted administrator, preacher and teacher of the word of God. He had some publications such as Asirin Aure in Hausa, Self on exile and other unpublished material and articles. He is a trained Children Teacher with the Child Evangelism Fellowship. He was ordained into the full gospel ministry of the Nigerian Baptist convention in 2007 during the convention General Session in Kubwa, Abuja. Currently, he is the Pastor of Salama Baptist Church, Buwaya of Gonin Gora Baptist Association, Kaduna Baptist Conference. Rev. John Oliver Yakubu is married to Pastor (Mrs) Sarah Yakubu and the marriage is blessed with a Daughter and grandchildren.

Brief Church History

Salama Baptist Church began in the year 1958. The Church started her Sunday worship service under a tree located on the piece of land donated to the church by Baba Ma'aji for the purpose of building the church auditorium. The following were the first worshippers: Baba Joji Barde, Late Zakwai Bagudu, Baba Isah Yerima, Late Jarfaru Dogo, Mama Kande Joji, Mama Asibi Bagudu, Late Gajere Yerima, Late Daniel Zarmai, Late Ladi Daniel, Late Ishaya Madami, Late Daniel Yerima and Late Bagudu Kwasau. The above members were going from Buwaya to Kakau station for worship service which seems some distance away. The first baptism into full membership of the church was conducted in the year 1968 by Rev. S.T Myers. The church was organized on the 3rd December, 1973 with 28 members under the leadership of Pastor D.H Maianguwa. A mission school was opened under the leadership of Pastor Kayit B. Duniya. Some preaching stations were also opened under the leadership of W.D Waziri, who also ordained the first set of deacons in 1998. The first church auditorium was built in 1959, the second in 1961; the third in 1980 and the fourth began in 2014 and still under construction with the capacity of 2500 worshippers conveniently. The Church runs two services: English and Hausa with an average attendance of 110 and 500 respectively.
